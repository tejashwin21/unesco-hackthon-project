InfoSphere

An Educative Platform of Media Literacy for Students and Youth

Learn More

The MIL Infosphere combines various tools like simplified information, chat features, quizzes, and fact-checkers, aimed at enhancing media and information literacy in an interactive and engaging way.

It provides users with clear, accessible, and easy-to-understand content about media and information literacy, helping them navigate the complexities of the digital world more effectively.

Through chat functions and quizzes, the Infosphere offers an engaging and interactive approach to learning, allowing users to test their knowledge and gain immediate feedback on MIL concepts.

The inclusion of a fact-checking feature ensures that users can verify information, promoting critical thinking and helping combat misinformation in the digital age.

Empowering Youth: UNESCO's MIL programs aim to empower young people in India to critically engage with media and information sources, promoting responsible digital citizenship. This is crucial in a country with a rapidly growing digital landscape and widespread use of social media among the youth.

Integration in Education: UNESCO is working with educational institutions in India to integrate MIL into curricula, helping students develop skills to navigate the complex media environment, identify misinformation, and understand the ethical implications of media consumption and production.

Collaborative Efforts: UNESCO partners with Indian government bodies, NGOs, and media organizations to implement MIL programs. These collaborations help reach students and youth in schools, colleges, and communities, fostering media literacy as an essential life skill.

Workshops and Campaigns: MIL initiatives include workshops, campaigns, and competitions aimed at increasing awareness among students and youth about the importance of media literacy. These activities promote critical thinking, fact-checking, and the responsible use of digital tools.
